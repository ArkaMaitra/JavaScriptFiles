"Conflict" is a 1983 stop-motion cartoon directed by Garri Bardin, a renowned Soviet and Russian animation director. The film is a Cold War allegory where two groups of matches, separated by an arbitrary boundary, escalate a minor dispute into a global conflict. The matchsticks are initially from the same matchbox, symbolizing the arbitrary nature of the conflict. The film culminates in a nuclear strike, leaving the matchsticks charred and lifeless. The film received a 7.8/10 rating on IMDb and was a huge success despite not being approved by the State Film Agency due to its pacifist message
